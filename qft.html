On the Code Golf and Coding Challenges Stack Exchange, there was a long standing open problem to Build a working game of Tetris in Conway's Game of Life . When I first saw this problem in high school, I completely disregarded it as something I could solve or contribute to. But it sat in the back of my head, nagging at me, and every now and then I'd check back in, and one day I noticed that there was actually a group of people working on a solution.

The plan was to abstract away from plain Conway's Game of Life using an existing pattern called the OTCA Metapixel, which allows individual metapixels to follow different simulation rules. Then, using the rule variations, simple wires and logic gates could be designed. Using the logic gates, more complex hardware necessary for a simple computer architecture could be built. Finally, tetris could be programmed in this computer's assembly language.

That was the plan, anyways. When I joined, there were still some missing logic gates, the existing adder was large, delay circuitry was inefficient, and there was little progress on the more complex circuits. I got myself heavily involved in the hardware design, and spearheaded the construction of the final computer.
